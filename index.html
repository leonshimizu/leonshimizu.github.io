About

(Sorry it's lengthy)

I graduated from high-school in 2017 and then went to college at Allegheny College to study Mechanical Engineering and play football. I learned a lot and had some great experiences but after my first year, my girlfriend got pregnant. As a result, we moved back home to Guam and stayed there until she gave birth. While I was there, I continued to study at the University of Guam and worked as an Engineering Intern at a company called Black Construction (Tutor Perini). Then after she gave birth, we moved to Las Vegas, NV to continue studying in the mainland. Maybe about a semester into school in Las Vegas, I began to realize that I don’t enjoy engineering, I just did it because I’ve liked math most of my life. As a result, I dropped out of school and began trying a couple different jobs. First I started with Vons Supermarket and worked there for about 3 months before I received the opportunity to work at Amazon. Then at Amazon, I started from the bottom, as a Tier 1 Picker. At the time I enjoyed it, the pay was decent and it was interesting to drive the machines around all day. From there I received my first promotion in about 5 months to become a Trainer. Then about 3 months later, received my next promotion to become an Assistant Manager. About 5 months into the role is when I began to start thinking about learning something new (something with computers). I then got into contact with a couple of my family members and talked to them about how they became Software Developers. That really interested me so I began researching everything I possibly could on software development. I learned mostly about a program with Amazon called the Amazon Technical Academy and learned about coding bootcamps. They both really peaked my interest but I ended up leaving Amazon and choosing Actualize Coding Bootcamp for a few reasons, time and sustainability. Now I’m studying with Actualize and I’m also about to study with Woz U as well. I’m learning a lot and really enjoy it. I’d say it’s one of the best career decisions I’ve made. Everyday I wake up looking forward to coding and how I can improve, maybe that’s because it’s fairly new still, but I really enjoy it and can definitely see myself being successful in this field.

    Strengths that I bring to the Software Development Field:
  • Empathy - Easily one of the most important things to me. I try to see the perspective of others to see how I can help them or how we can better work together to find a solution or whatever it may be.
  • Leadership - At my previous job at Amazon, one of the things I prided mysel with was treating others how I want to be treated, I know that’s something that seems like it’s common sense, but I really tried to live that and lead by example.
  • Adaptive - I make mistakes, same as everyone but I really try to learn from my mistakes and the mistakes of others so I can grow, become better, and not make those same mistakes again.
  • Work Ethic - I take pride in my work and that means, I will 100% give my all in anything and everything I do, it doesn’t matter if it seems like it’s something small or something big, I will give it my all.
